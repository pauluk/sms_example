ACCESS THE SMS APPLICATION

Getting Started

Navigate to the Gov SMS App at poc.sms.risen108010.co.uk

You'll see the landing page with the option to Send bulk Text messages.

This application uses the GOV.UK Notify service to send SMS messages securely.
5

SIGN IN TO YOUR ACCOUNT

Enter your sign in details in the login form.

Click the Sign In button to authenticate and access the bulk SMS features.

Only authorised users with valid credentials can access the SMS sending functionality.
6

SELECT BULK SEND

From the main dashboard, click on "Bulk Send" to access the bulk messaging feature.

This allows you to send multiple SMS messages at once using a CSV file.

Bulk Send is ideal for sending notifications to multiple recipients efficiently.
7

DOWNLOAD THE TEMPLATE

Click "Download Template" to get the CSV template file.

You can either complete this template with your recipient data, or use a previously prepared file.

The template ensures your data is formatted correctly for bulk sending.
8

UPLOAD YOUR CSV FILE

Click the file upload area and select your completed bulk-sms-template.csv file.

Once uploaded, the file name will be displayed confirming successful selection.

Ensure your CSV contains valid phone numbers and message content before uploading.
9

PREVIEW YOUR MESSAGES

Important Step

Preview and review all the messages that will be sent.

This screen shows each recipient's phone number and the message content they will receive.

Always verify message content and recipient numbers before sending.
10

SEND YOUR MESSAGES

Once you've reviewed and are satisfied with the messages, click "Send X Messages" to dispatch them.

In this example, we're sending 2 messages to the recipients listed.

This action cannot be undone. Ensure all details are correct before sending.
11

CONFIRMATION RECEIVED

Success!

A toast notification appears confirming your messages have been sent successfully.

In this example: "Successfully sent 2 messages"

Messages are queued and sent via GOV.UK Notify's secure infrastructure.
